Why Washington Beer?

要日本語テキスト
Washington produces some of the finest beer in the United States. Its 150+ breweries rank 2nd only behind California in the number of active breweries. With the large number of breweries operating in a state with a modest population, breweries must be of the highest quality to survive in such a competitive market. Despite the competition, neighboring breweries have an unrivaled camaraderie as they continue to grow both locally and beyond.

No other brewing area in the United States can claim fresher ingredients than Washington, as the majority of the hops grown in the US come from Washington hop fields. In fact, 75% of the hops grown in the United States come from Washington’s Yakima Valley, making it one of the most important hop growing regions in the world.

With local hops abundant, it is no surprise Washington brewers make very hop-forward beers. As early pioneers and innovators of the micro brewery boom in the 1980’s, Washington brewers were among the first to brew hoppy beers - beginning with amber and brown ales and ESB, then eventually spearheading the craft movement with the re-imagination of India Pale Ale using generous amounts of North American hops grown locally in Washington. Washington innovators like Diamond Knot set a trend that continues today, with IPA now the #1 produced craft beer in America. These original and refreshing Northwestern Ales are incredibly well known and revered in Washington, with 1 in every 4 beers drank there being a craft beer. Locals there have been keeping a secret – one that Evergreen Imports wants to share with Japan.

Washington is also an important producer of malt for brewing grains, being the 4th largest producer in the US. Washington’s pristine water comes from the Cascade mountains and other local ranges, ensuring that these three staple ingredients to quality beer are the freshest anywhere – a claim that no other region can truly make.
