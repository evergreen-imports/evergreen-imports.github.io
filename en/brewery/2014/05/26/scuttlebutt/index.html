ScuttleButt Brewing Co

Knowing her husband hated running out of beer in the house, Cynthia “Scuttle” bought husband Phil a homebrewing kit for Father’s Day in 1990. As thoughtful as the gift was, it quickly became a source of strife as Phil wanted to use the kitchen for making beer while Scuttle wanted to use it for preparing family meals. In order to maintain martial bliss, Phil needed to take his passion for brewing out of the house, so he did – in a big way. In 1995 Phil made the jump to the big-time, going from his 5 gallon (20 liter) kitchen batches to 20 barrels (2450 liters)!
In 2011, with the help of their many loyal customers, Scuttlebutt reached over 6000 barrels sold... the rough equivalent of 186,000 gallons of beer, or 700,000 liters – ranking them in the top 100 microbreweries in America in production.
